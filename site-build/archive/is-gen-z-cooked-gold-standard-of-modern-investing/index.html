Is Gen Z cooked - Gold Standard of Modern Investing

2025-04-23

Originally published 2025-04-23 on the site's earlier chapter as a crypto/Web3 blog. Kept as-is for the record.

Every generation has its go-to asset.

For your grandparents, it was fixed deposits, earning a rock-solid 15% returns.
Your parents swore by gold and real estate. This is back when land was cheap and 24k was sold at local grocery shops (okay, that was an exaggeration)

Millennials? They rode the equity wave, throwing SIPs on 1st of every month.

Now it's your turn!
You're earning your first salary. Scrolling through apps that give you access to every asset class imaginable. Stocks. Crypto. Bonds. Art. Real estate tokens. Banana futures, maybe?

But each of them comes with a caveat. A risk. A red flag. Something that makes you pause.

So, what now?

Should you even invest as a Gen Z?

I recently had a word with a cousin who has just entered the workforce.

I asked him about investment and his response was hilarious. He told me that our generation (and the previous) invested because we didn't have anything fun to spend on!

You can't argue with that logic, can you?

I sort of agree with him but there are some glaring reasons why GenZ s should definitely invest. Here is a quick rundown.

1. Time

Unlike previous generations, Gen Z isn’t entering adulthood carrying the weight of EMIs, joint families, or urgent retirement plans for their parents.
Their parents handled their own financial well-being pretty decently.

Which means: Gen Z gets a rare luxury. Time.

The kind of time millennials wish they had when compounding finally made sense to them.
Even modest investments today can snowball into serious wealth tomorrow.

2. Diversification Pro Max

Gen Z isn’t limited to buying physical gold or opening a demat account with a broker who still calls you before buying/selling.

From real estate to REITs, stocks to ETFs, digital gold to gold-backed tokens, if there’s an asset class, there’s an app for it.

You don’t just invest anymore. You customize your portfolio.

3. Invest or Die

Back in the day, your grandparents earned 14–15% on fixed deposits.

Today? Leave your money idle in a savings account, and it quietly loses 6–7% a year.
That’s erosion of finances.

In a world where everything from coffee to cab rides gets costlier every quarter, not investing is the fastest way to fall behind.

But Where?

This is the part where the topic of this post makes much more sense.

You are not cooked because of lack of information but the overload of it.

You’ve got a hundred YouTube bros screaming “multibagger!” in your left ear, and thousands Instagram finfluencers selling you “the next big thing” in your right.

Call me old school, but there is a saying I have always lived by:

When in doubt, zoom out!

Blockchain backed assets (be it gold or something else) have one feature that stands out! Trust.

In July 2024, the Indian government reduced the import duty on gold from 15% to 6%. This policy change led to a significant drop in domestic gold prices—approximately 5%—which directly impacted the redemption value of SGBs maturing around that time. For instance, the SGBs issued in August 2016 were redeemed at ₹6,938 per gram, which was about 4.5% lower than the average price before the duty cut.

This sudden policy shift highlighted a vulnerability in SGBs: their redemption value is tied to the prevailing gold price, which can be influenced by government policies and international market dynamics. In contrast, PAXG holders experienced real-time price adjustments, allowing for more agile investment decisions.
