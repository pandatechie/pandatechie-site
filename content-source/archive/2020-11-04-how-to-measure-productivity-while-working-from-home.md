A few days ago I covered a tech backed methodology called [Pomodoro technique](https://pandatechie.in/2020/11/02/pomodoro-technique/), to **boost your productivity**. Next logical step in this sequence is to **how to measure productivity** while working from home . With each **five** **minute** conversation over lunch/nicotine breaks, turning into a **30 minute teams meeting**, it is very important to understand how much time you leave for yourself to **focus on the work.**

![How to measure productivity- My analytics](https://www.it.miami.edu/_assets/images/ms-myanalytics.jpg)

My Analytics by Microsoft

With so much to accomplish amidst so many **distractions**, there has to be a way I can track all these key metrics. Well, there is! Enter, **[Microsoft MyAnalytics](https://myanalytics.microsoft.com/?v=home)**. I'll be honest, **Microsoft sends you such analysis every week** but since it goes into the 'others' inbox, I ignored it. After I saw it once, I found it really useful.

## What is MyAnalytics?

MyAnalytics is a tool from Microsoft that helps you get some **insights on the time spent on meetings and collaboration via emails** and **slots** dedicated to '**focus**'. Therefore, helping you plan your time wisely and reduce non productive meetings. Available for all office 365 users, tool leverages the **tightly integrated** ecosystem of **Teams**, **Outlook**, **Sharepoint** and **OneDrive**.

This personalized dashboard**, also shares insights on the time spent on collaboration outside work hours**. Let's dive into each of the features of the tool individually.

## Measuring Productivity using MyAnlytics:

## 1. Focus Time:

![How to measure productivity by using focus time](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/image-1.png)

Time Available to Focus

A simple **pie chart** in this section tells you how much of your time is **left to focus** on your tasks **outside calls, meetings and emails**. This functionality will also help you to block certain time of the day for your tasks. This will show as 'Focus Time' on your calendar if anyone tries to block it.

![How to measure productivity by blocking focus time. ](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/image-2.png)

Section which involves planning focus time everyday.

## 2. Wellbeing:

With increased focus on employee wellbeing, this feature aims to measure the days/hours you were able to **disconnect**. These precious hours are called **'Quiet Hours**'. This section would measure the hours where there was no significant work (mails/calls) outside your work hours (weekends including).

![How to measure productivity: measuring quite hours](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/image-4.png)

Number of quiet days outside working hours

![How to measure productivity: measuring quite hours](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/image-5.png)

Trend of quiet hours

This section is a real eye opener. It would go to the extent of telling us, what causes **disruption in our silent hour**s. Also, it will tell you the people who you spend most time with, outside work hours.

![How to measure productivity: measuring quite hours disruptions](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/image-3.png)

Disruptions in the silent hours

This functionality would also **encourage you to reduce outflow of emails** during non working hours by telling you the trend. Be the change you want to see in the world (organization)

![How to measure productivity by reducing quite hour disruption](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/image-6.png)

Trend of disrupting someone else's quite hours

## 3. Network

This one is a really nice to understand our network in workplace. It will tell the the number of people you have met/mailed in past.

![Measuring productivity by understanding collaborators](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/image-7.png)

Number of collaborators

In a very neat map view, it will tell me the collaborators with filters such as active/external/new and important (I presume they are the ones which are top collaborators).

![](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/image-8.png)

My Network Map

## Conclusion:

I think it is a good eye opener of a tool. I never knew certain data points which this tool helped me gather.
