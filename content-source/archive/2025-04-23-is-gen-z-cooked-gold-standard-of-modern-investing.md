You can't argue with that logic, can you?

I sort of agree with him but there are some glaring reasons why GenZ s should definitely invest. Here is a quick rundown.

### 1. Time

Unlike previous generations, Gen Z isn’t entering adulthood carrying the weight of EMIs, joint families, or urgent retirement plans for their parents.  
Their parents handled their own financial well-being pretty decently.

Which means: Gen Z gets a rare luxury. Time**.**

The kind of time millennials wish they had when compounding finally made sense to them.  
Even modest investments today can snowball into serious wealth tomorrow.

### 2. Diversification Pro Max

Gen Z isn’t limited to buying physical gold or opening a demat account with a broker who still calls you before buying/selling.

From real estate to REITs, stocks to ETFs, digital gold to gold-backed tokens, if there’s an asset class, there’s an app for it.

You don’t just invest anymore. You customize your portfolio.

### 3. Invest or Die

Back in the day, your grandparents earned 14–15% on fixed deposits.

Today? Leave your money idle in a savings account, and it quietly loses 6–7% a year.  
That’s erosion of finances.

In a world where everything from coffee to cab rides gets costlier every quarter, not investing is the fastest way to fall behind.

Blockchain backed assets (be it gold or something else) have one feature that stands out! Trust.

In July 2024, the Indian government reduced the import duty on gold from 15% to 6%. This policy change led to a significant drop in domestic gold prices—approximately 5%—which directly impacted the redemption value of SGBs maturing around that time. For instance, the SGBs issued in August 2016 were redeemed at ₹6,938 per gram, which was about 4.5% lower than the average price before the duty cut.

This sudden policy shift highlighted a vulnerability in SGBs: their redemption value is tied to the prevailing gold price, which can be influenced by government policies and international market dynamics.
