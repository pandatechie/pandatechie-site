Now that we have the basics sorted, let's find out what actually is meant by **different types of cryptocurrency** (as mentioned, more accurate term would be **crypto** and not '**currency**'). When people generally ask this question, they are basically **referring** to the different type of **tokens** out there. Why? Because there's a subtle difference between the two. (Explained [here](https://pandatechie.in/2021/11/22/what-is-the-difference-between-coin-and-token/)) So in today's blogpost we are going to talk about just that. **What are different types of cryptocurrency or to be technically correct, different types of tokens out there?**

## A. Platform Tokens:

These are the tokens which are created to **support** a **decentralized application**. For example, [Uniswap](https://pandatechie.in/2021/10/15/what-is-uniswap-and-uni-token/) is a decentralized exchange meant for swapping different ERC20 tokens. However, Uniswap also his it's own token called **UNI**. Right now UNI has **no specific use case** and is intended to be a governance token (more on it in a while), but it is handed out to people who invest in the **liquidity pools** and later can be used for [yield farming](https://pandatechie.in/2021/10/23/what-is-yield-farming-in-crypto/) etc.

![Uniswap Logo](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/uniswap-1024x614.jpg)

## B. Wrapped Tokens:

For the normies who are well versed with stock markets, think of these like [ETFs](https://pandatechie.in/2020/10/22/is-it-the-right-time-to-buy-gold-how-to-invest-in-gold/). Imagine that you plan to buy **gold** but don't have the **money** to **buy** an **entire gram** (or whatever the minimum unit is). In that case, you could **purchase** a **tokenized** form of gold for as **low as Rs. 100**. That way you don't have to care about **minimum investment** and other factors like **storage**, **counterfeits** etc.

![Different types of Cryptocurrency: Wrapped Token](https://pandatechie.in/wp-content/uploads/2021/11/image-30.png)

Now take that example and expand it to all the **assets** you could think of. **Silver**, **Copper**, **Iron** etc. etc. So basically you could **WRAP** an asset in a packet and **trade** using it **securely** through blockchain.

## C. Stable Coins:

Well, this is another blogpost in itself and guess what? I did write about it a few days ago. [Here's](https://pandatechie.in/2021/10/24/what-is-stablecoin-types-of-stablecoin-and-uses/) where you can explore more.

![Different types of Cryptocurrency: Stablecoin](https://forumpay.com/d_content/Las-stablecoins-pueden-ser-una-defensa-contra-la-inflacion.jpeg)

For the rest of us, **stable coins** are mostly like wrapped tokens but **instead** of a **commodity** they **mimic** the price of a **fiat** **currency**. This could be Dollar, **Yen**, **Euro**, you get the idea. Some of the examples of stable coins would be DAI, USDT, USDC etc.

## D. Utility Token:

I will instead start with an example which would instantly make it clear. There's a Bangalore based startup called [Drife](https://www.drife.io/). It's a **Decentralized Uber** based on **Blockchain**. The $**DRIFE** token is used to **pay** the **drivers** for rides.

Another one? There's a **metaverse** project called **Decentraland**. People use the currency of that **metaverse** called $**MANA** to **purchase** virtual land.

I hope you get the idea. Utility coin is something that has a **purpose** within the **project ecosystem**. This purpose could be anything **tangible** in nature.

![Different types of Cryptocurrency: Utility Coin](https://miro.medium.com/max/830/1*daXNKxGj6nlVtZiGQjbjxw.png)

Oh, and if you still haven't figured out, **Fuel Coins** are the biggest examples of utility tokens. For example **ETH, SOL** are used as **gas money** for all **transactions** on **Ethereum Mainnet** and **Solana** blockchains respectively.

## E. Governance:

Are you into stock market? If yes, then you must be aware about the **annual general meetings** companies conduct regularly. As a **share holder** you are free to **join** these meetings and also **vote** on certain decisions. ***More the number of shares you hold, greater is the value of your vote*** OR so to say, 1 Share = 1 Vote.

![Different types of Cryptocurrency: Governance](https://assets-global.website-files.com/5f5504366445c3972f45286e/5fd3b6ade5a9dcbad51a69d5_201210%20Illustrations_Price%20Change%20Logos.jpg)

In a crypto parlance, this is what a **governance** **token** can achieve. In the example that I quoted above, **UNI** might take the shape of a **governance token** in the future. So if one fine day **Uniswap decides** to **increase** the **swapping fee** from **0.3%** to **0.6%**, these token holders will be able to vote on that decision.
